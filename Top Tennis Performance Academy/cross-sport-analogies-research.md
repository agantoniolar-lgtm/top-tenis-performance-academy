# Cross-Domain Analogies for Top Tennis

**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Inspiration:** *Range* by David Epstein, chapter on the power of analogies (Kepler, targeted radiation)
**Question behind this document:** What mature practices exist *outside* tennis for certifying evidence of talent, and which ones are stealable for Top Tennis?
**Domains researched:**
1. High school to NCAA football recruiting pipeline
2. Admissions to music conservatories and ballet/performing arts schools

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The two reference classes converge on a surprisingly similar architecture, and that convergence is the strongest validation of Top Tennis's thesis: **what's scarce isn't the data, it's the verifiability of the data.**

Both worlds learned this the hard way. In football, SPARQ (Nike) died because its proprietary formula was never public. Signing Day Sports (NYSE American: SGN until March 2026, ticker AIB after the BlockchAIn Inc. merger) replaced the category by positioning explicitly as "video-verified data" after coaches stopped trusting self-reported times. In conservatories, Juilliard and Curtis pre-screening videos explicitly forbid editing and reverb. In ballet, Royal Ballet School writes in their FAQs: "we're looking for potential, not perfection."

On top of that shared foundation, the two worlds solved problems adjacent to yours:

1. **Mature NCAA recruiting:** standardized highlight reels, verified measurables, multi-source consensus star ratings, structured letters, sponsored events (Nike The Opening, Rivals Camps). Platforms: Hudl (B2B-school, $900–$1,600/year), NCSA (B2C-parent, $1,500–$3,000), FieldLevel (coach-to-coach), Signing Day Sports (video-verified).

2. **Certification of intangibles without an objective ranking:** double filter of pre-screening + live audition, longitudinal annual jury, applicant-signed statement of purpose, direct recommender uploads, multi-tier donor recognition, performance psychology integrated into the file. Platforms: Acceptd, DecisionDesk.

The non-obvious part — the "Range-style" analogies, the ones that only show up when you leave tennis — sits at the **intersection**: the mature evidence format comes from football (Talent Card, highlight reel, measurables); the format for certifying intangibles comes from conservatories (longitudinal jury, recommender upload, statement of purpose). **Nobody has crossed the two.** Top Tennis can be the first.

If I had to compress the thesis into one sentence: *the Talent Card is Acceptd's digital portfolio with Signing Day Sports' verified-measurables rigor, assembled longitudinally with Juilliard Pre-College's jury cadence.*

## 3. The most "Range-style" analogies (non-obvious)

These are the 6 ideas you wouldn't surface by researching other tennis academies. They're what makes the exercise worth doing.

### A. Juilliard Pre-College's longitudinal annual jury

Every Juilliard Pre-College student passes through a jury at the end of the spring semester in front of a faculty panel. They receive **an averaged grade + written comments within 2 weeks**. The grade is final and goes on the transcript. ABT JKO delivers **two written reports per year** (mid-year evaluation + curriculum assessment).

*Why it's non-obvious for tennis:* in tennis, no "transcript" exists. UTR is a constantly-shifting score with no signed document. An annual jury with panel-signed report builds exactly what NCAA recruiters want to see (longitudinal evolution with accredited human voice) and currently don't receive.

*Mapping to your platform:* the annual jury is the backbone of the longitudinal file for junior players (8–17 years old). Head coach + 2 specialists. Signed report into the file. Sustained accumulation = evidence that can't be faked at the last minute.

### B. The audition as a ballet class, not a performance

Royal Ballet School doesn't audition by asking applicants to "show me your best variation." They audition by giving them a class. The panel observes **how the applicant takes corrections** in real time. That lets them see coachability — the one thing a highlight reel can never show.

*Why it's non-obvious for tennis:* academies show "exhibition matches" to sponsors and recruiters. The format is performance, not diagnostic. The coachability signal is lost.

*Mapping to your platform:* for sponsors and recruiters, offer an "open practice session" where the athlete receives coaching corrections live. The session gets recorded, attached to the file, and becomes an evaluable artifact. Much richer than a match.

### C. Recommender uploads directly (the athlete doesn't see the letter)

In Acceptd, recommenders upload their letters and reports directly to the platform. The applicant doesn't see or edit them. This workflow detail guarantees credibility: the recruiter knows the letter wasn't ghost-written by the parent.

*Why it's non-obvious for tennis:* in sports, specialist reports typically pass through the academy (which can filter or edit them). In arts admissions, this is structurally forbidden.

*Mapping to your platform:* coach, psychologist, nutritionist, physio all upload reports directly to the platform with their own accounts. The academy doesn't edit or filter. This turns the Specialist Reports epic into a verifiable asset, not disguised marketing collateral.

### D. Blind review (orchestra blind auditions → Goldin & Rouse, NBER w5903)

Blind auditions in symphony orchestras increased women's probability of advancing in preliminary rounds by 50%, and "severalfold" in final rounds. The switch to blind auditions explains 30%–55% of the rise in women among new hires since 1970. The canonical Goldin & Rouse paper was later questioned methodologically, but the operating principle was absorbed by the music world: the screen solves panel bias.

*Why it's non-obvious for tennis:* nobody in youth sports offers blind review as a feature.

*Mapping to your platform:* the university recruiter can activate "blind mode" on the Talent Card during initial shortlisting — hides name, photo, academy, country. Only the record and metrics are visible. A differentiating feature for NCAA universities worried about Title IX and equity audits. Better to design it from day one than retrofit when they ask for it in 3 years.

### E. Multi-tier donor recognition + matching gifts (ABT JKO model)

ABT JKO received $2M from an anonymous donor, **half of it used as a 2:1 match for the Friends of JKO Challenge.** Donor recognition includes listing in every ABT Playbill + recognition at JKO performances + invitations to events with students in training. The Jerome Robbins Foundation communicates with semi-annual newsletters carrying "artist's journey" narratives, not dashboards.

*Why it's non-obvious for tennis:* sports sponsorship in LatAm is dominated by the "logo on the shirt" or "exhibition appearance" model. Arts donor recognition is a totally different paradigm: it turns the sponsor into an *author* of the story, not a billboard.

*Mapping to your platform:* Sponsor Portal with visible tiers + explicit matching mechanic + narrative semi-annual newsletter. The sponsor doesn't get metrics — they get a story. That lets you charge more and retain longer.

### F. Statement of purpose signed by the applicant

Both Juilliard and Royal Academy of Music require the statement of purpose to be signed by the applicant — not the coach, not the parent. It's the applicant's voice in first person. If it's adult-written, panels can smell it and discount it.

*Why it's non-obvious for tennis:* sports files normally have the athlete's bio written by the academy. The athlete's own voice doesn't appear.

*Mapping to your platform:* the athlete's post-tournament voice (the PTF you already have in your vision) is exactly this. The validation is that conservatories have been doing this for 50 years and it works. Reinforces: the PTF is not an optional feature, it's a structural column of the differentiation.

## 5. Traps inherited from both worlds (what NOT to do)

1. **Don't replicate the coach-monopoly dependency.** Curtis Institute (Brodsky, 1985–86), Eastman (Varon, 2023), Royal Conservatory Toronto (Biss resigned in protest) — all cases where the teacher as sole gateway produced abuse. The Top Tennis coach is *one* authorized signature, not the only one. Specialist reports + athlete voice are structural counterweights, not optional features.

2. **Don't use an opaque proprietary formula.** SPARQ (Nike) died because its formula was never public. If Top Tennis creates an "internal index," document the methodology openly or use multi-source consensus. The 247Sports Composite lesson is this.

3. **Don't imitate NCSA-style predatory contracts.** NCSA was criticized for long contracts with hidden penalties, sales-pitch "false urgency," and the NY AG vs National Scouting Report settlement is the legal precedent. Top Tennis: month-to-month subscription, clear cancellation, no scholarship promises.

4. **Don't replicate the economic filter of conservatories.** Training at Juilliard/Curtis costs hundreds of thousands; New Republic and Portside document how this pushes out non-privileged families. The Talent Card must be a visibility path for athletes who can't afford master classes, not a new paywall.

5. **Don't accept cinematic highlights as evidence.** Reverb, editing, and music drops are explicitly penalized in conservatory pre-screening. If Top Tennis accepts edited video, it kills its own differentiation against YouTube highlights that already exist.

6. **Don't underestimate letter/report inflation.** Without cross-verifiability (public profile of the signer + clickable track record + temporal specificity), coach reports devalue within 12–18 months.

7. **Don't design the Sponsor Portal as a dashboard.** Jerome Robbins and ABT JKO won donors with narrative, not metrics. The sponsor wants to be an author, not an auditor.

8. **Don't make the jury a ceremonial annual ritual.** Juilliard's jury works because the grade is final and goes on the transcript. If Top Tennis's jury is decorative, recruiters will figure it out in 2 cycles and stop weighting it.

9. **Don't confuse visibility with relationship.** PrepHero, BeRecruited and dozens more promised "mass exposure" without delivering. University coaches don't mine these platforms — they consult them when they already know the athlete. The Talent Card is an asset, not a discovery funnel.

10. **Don't skip blind review.** The moment you sell to NCAA universities, blind review goes from optional feature to requirement (Title IX, equity audits). Easier to design from day one than retrofit later.

## 6. Product decisions that follow from this research

Synthesizing all of it, the concrete decisions this exercise recommends:

1. **The Talent Card is a persistent account from age 8, not a PDF generated at the moment.** It accumulates reports longitudinally. The recruiter accesses it via authorized link.

2. **There are 4 authorized signers in the file, not 1.** Coach (technical), Psychologist, Nutritionist/Physio/S&C, Athlete (via PTF). Each uploads directly, without academy editing. This is the structural counterweight to Curtis/Eastman.

3. **There is a mandatory annual jury.** Panel-signed report (coach + 2 specialists) at the end of each competitive cycle, within 2 weeks. It enters the file as a "transcript."

4. **Every metric that appears on the Talent Card is time-stamped and verified by the academy.** Serve speed recorded, not self-reported. This is the Signing Day Sports principle.

5. **The pre-screening video is structured.** It's not a free highlight reel. It must cover: serve + forehand under pressure + backhand under pressure + recovery + post-tournament voice. No editing.

6. **The public coach profile includes alumni placement.** NCAA universities + ATP historical rankings. This turns the coach's word into signal with weight. It's the Dorothy DeLay model.

7. **The Sponsor Portal is narrative newsletter + matching gifts, not dashboard.** Semi-annual communication with "athlete's journey" narrative. 2:1 matching mechanic with deadline.

8. **Blind review is available from day one** for recruiters who activate it during shortlisting.

9. **There is a formal rubric for any human evaluation** (5 criteria × 3 levels + 2+ independent evaluators). That reduces inter-judge variance documented in the music literature.

10. **The PTF (athlete's voice) is not an optional feature.** It's a structural column, validated by 50 years of conservatories requiring an applicant-signed statement of purpose.
